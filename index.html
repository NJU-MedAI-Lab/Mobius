ℳöbius Framework

Closed-Loop Mutual Supervision for Reliable Medical Vision–Language Reasoning

Overview

The Möbius Framework addresses hallucination and modality laziness in medical large vision-language models (LVLMs) by establishing a bidirectional, closed-loop supervision between an LVLM and a Segment Anything Model (SAM).

Language from the LVLM guides anatomical segmentation, while segmentation from SAM grounds visual reasoning—enabling annotation-efficient alignment of perception and semantics.

Core Components

ℳöbiusMed-LVLM

A medical LVLM trained within the Möbius framework, producing visually grounded diagnostic descriptions with reduced hallucination.

ℳöbiusMed-SAM

A vision foundation model enhanced by linguistic guidance from the LVLM, capable of interpreting free-form medical instructions.

Key Innovation

Unlike conventional pipelines that treat vision and language modules independently, Möbius creates a self-correcting loop:

  • LVLM → SAM: Natural language prompts refine region-of-interest segmentation.
  • SAM → LVLM: Precise masks constrain visual grounding, suppressing unfaithful generation.

This mutual supervision mimics the Möbius strip—seamlessly connecting two modalities into a unified, self-reinforcing system.
